Connecting storage devices

Connecting an HD (hard drive) or SSD (solid state drive) to the motherboard is an essential task when assembling or update a computer.


Before you start, have at hand:

A screwdriver compatible with the case screws.
The SATA data cable (usually comes with the motherboard or disk).
The SATA power cable (part of the computer's power supply).


HD or SSD: Locate the device that will be installed. HDDs are generally larger and heavier, while SSDs have smaller form factors (2.5", M.2 or NVMe).
SATA port: For 2.5" or 3.5" HDs and SSDs, you will need to connect a SATA cable. These doors are in motherboard and are generally identified as "SATA 1", "SATA 2", etc.
M.2 port: If the SSD is of the M.2 type, it will be connected directly to an M.2 slot on the motherboard.


Position and connect cables



For SATA HDs or SSDs

Mount the device in the case:
Use the appropriate screws to fix the HD or SSD in the specific compartment of the case.
Connect the SATA data cable:
Insert one end of the SATA cable into the HD or SSD port and the other end into the SATA port on the motherboard.
Connect the power cord:
Take a SATA connector from the power supply and connect it to the HD or SSD.
Organize cables:
Use clips or cable ties to keep cables organized and out of the way.


For M.2 SSDs

Locate the M.2 slot on the motherboard:
The slot can be next to the processor or between the PCIe slots.
Remove the slot screw:
Loosen the small screw that holds the SSD in place.
Insert SSD:
Place the SSD into the slot at a 30Â° angle and gently press it down.
Fix the SSD:
Replace the screw to secure the SSD in place.
