HD and SSD

Storage devices store data permanently or temporarily on computers and electronic devices. They can be primary, for data in immediate use, or secondary, such as HDs and SSDs, which store files and programs permanently.

HDs store data on spinning magnetic disks, while SSDs use partless flash memory mobile devices, providing faster and more reliable access. Both have distinct performance characteristics and durability.

HDs offer greater capacity at a lower cost, but are slower and more susceptible to failure due to moving parts. SSDs are faster and more resilient, but more expensive and have a limited lifespan in terms of of recordings.

In addition to HDs and SSDs, other storage devices such as CDs, DVDs and memory cards serve different needs, such as backup or data transport. The choice depends on capacity, price and desired performance.
