Placing the processor on the motherboard

Placing the processor on the motherboard is a simple process, but requires care. Here is a summary of the main steps:

Prepare the environment: Make sure you are in a clean area and wear an anti-static wrist strap to prevent component damage.

Place the motherboard on a table.

Lift the socket lever: The motherboard socket has a lever that must be lifted to make room for the processor.

Align the processor: Check the alignment marker on the processor (a triangle or dot) and on the socket to ensure the processor is being placed in the correct position.

Place the processor: With the processor aligned, gently place it into the socket. It should fit without forcing.

Close the lever: Lower the lever to secure the processor in place.

Apply thermal paste (if necessary): If the processor does not have thermal paste pre-applied, place a small amount on the chip.

Install the cooler: Place the cooler over the processor and secure it correctly to ensure adequate cooling.

With this, the first step towards an assembled computer will be complete.
