Preparing the clothing

First step:

To build a computer safely, the choice of clothing is important to avoid damage to the computer. sensitive components. Wear comfortable clothing, preferably made from natural materials, such as cotton, which generate less static electricity. Fabrics such as wool and polyester can accumulate static, which which increases the risk of electrostatic discharge that can damage electronic components.

Second step:

Avoid wearing metallic accessories such as bracelets, rings or watches, as these items can generate sparks or even accidentally touching sensitive parts of the computer. If possible, keep your hands free from jewelry or other objects that could interfere with assembly.

Third step:

To further reduce the risk of static electricity, it is recommended to wear an anti-static wrist strap. This bracelet must be connected to a grounded metal surface, helping to dissipate the charge static accumulated in the body. In addition, choose shoes with rubber soles, which help to isolate the body and reduce the risk of discharges.

Fourth step:

Always opt for simple and comfortable clothes, without materials that generate static, and avoid accessories metallic. This clothing care protects both you and the components, ensuring that assembly is carried out safely and accurately.
