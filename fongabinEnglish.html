Placing the power supply in the cabinet


Installing the power supply (PSU) in the case is one of the initial steps in assembling a computer.
For this:
Work on a flat, clean, static-free surface.
Wear an anti-static wrist strap or regularly touch a grounded metal surface.
Make sure the cabinet is open and ready for installation.


Identify the position of the source

Check the designated location for the power supply on the case, which is usually at the bottom back (mostly of modern cabinets) or at the top back (on older models).

Observe the ventilation space:
If the case has openings or filters at the bottom, position the power supply fan downwards.
Otherwise, position the fan upwards, pointing into the case.


Prepare and install the font

Check that the source is in the correct voltage mode (110V or 220V) according to the outlet used to supply power to the PC, if it is not automatic bivolt.
Organize the source cables to facilitate later connection.


Position the power supply: Place it in the appropriate bay, aligning the screw holes on the back of the cabinet.
Secure the power supply: Use the screws provided with the power supply or case to secure it to the case. Normally, they are four screws positioned in the corners.


Organize and connect cables (optional)

Before connecting cables to the motherboard and other components, organize them for easier management and maintenance. adequate airflow within the case.
Use clips or cable ties to secure the cables, if necessary.


Although connecting the cables can be done later, you can take advantage of this moment to connect the power main to the motherboard, CPU, GPU and other devices.
