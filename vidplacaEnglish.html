Placing the video card on the motherboard

Placing the video card on the motherboard is a simple process, but requires care. Here is a summary of the steps main:

Wear an anti-static wrist strap or touch a metal surface to discharge static electricity before handling the internal components.

Then, locate the PCI-Express x16 slot on the motherboard. This is the largest slot and is where the video card will be installed. Most video cards use this type of slot.

In order for the video card to have room to fit, you will need to remove one or more expansion caps on the rear of the cabinet. Remove the screws holding the cover and remove it. Normally, the lids are located just below the PCI-Express slot.

Take the video card and align the gold connectors (PCI-Express) with the PCI-Express x16 slot on the motherboard. The graphics card is uniquely shaped, so it will only fit one way.

With the card well aligned, carefully insert it into the PCI-Express slot. The plate should slide smoothly to the bottom of the slot. Apply light pressure to ensure the graphics card seats completely. You may hear a "click" when the video card is properly installed.

Make sure your power supply is sufficient to support the video card. Most powerful cards require higher capacity power supplies. If your graphics card is large, check the available space in your case before purchasing to ensure it it fits. Drivers: After installation, always install the latest graphics card drivers to ensure the best performance and compatibility with games and software.
