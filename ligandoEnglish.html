Turning on the computer for the first time


Check all cables


Before turning on the computer, make sure all cables are properly connected. This includes:
Power cable from source to socket.
Power cable from power supply to motherboard and CPU.
SATA data cable (if using HDDs or SSDs) connected to the motherboard.
Video connections (HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI) between the video card or motherboard and the monitor.
Front panel connections (power button, LEDs, USB, audio).


Check the components


Check that all components are well fitted and installed:
Motherboard: Check that the motherboard is correctly fixed in the case and that the cables are connected to the right places.
Processor (CPU): Make sure the cooler is securely attached to the processor.
RAM Memory: Check that the memory modules are securely seated in the appropriate slots.
Video card (if present): Make sure the video card is properly installed in the PCIe slot and that power cords (if necessary) are connected.
Storage devices: Check if the HD or SSD is correctly installed and connected.


Turn on the computer


Now that everything is checked, it's time to turn on the computer for the first time:
Press the power button on the case.
The computer should begin the boot process. You will see the LEDs light up and the fans start to rotate. The monitor screen can display POST (Power-On Self-Test) information, which is the power-on test.
