Connecting the cables


Power supply cable (PSU)

Power supply cable: The main cable coming from the power supply (PSU) must be connected to the power outlet power supply or a surge protector/line filter.
PSU power cable to motherboard: Locate the 24-pin connector (usually the largest connector) on the power supply and connect it to the motherboard, on the 24-pin power connector.
CPU Connector (4/8-pin): The power supply also has a 4- or 8-pin cable (depending on your motherboard) for power the CPU. Connect it to the CPU power connector on the motherboard.


SATA cables (For HD, SSD and Optical Drives)

SATA data cable: Connect a SATA cable from the motherboard to each storage drive (HD or SSD). The connectors SATA on the motherboard are usually located near the right edge.
SATA power cable: Connect a SATA power cable from the power supply to the storage drives. THE source must have specific connectors for this, generally black and flat.


Connect the Video Cable (To Video Card or Monitor)

Dedicated graphics card (GPU): If you are using a dedicated graphics card, insert the video cable (HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI, or VGA) from your GPU to the corresponding port on the monitor.
Integrated video (if not using a dedicated video card): If you are using the motherboard's integrated video, connect the video cable directly into the video output port on the motherboard (usually located on the back of the computer) cabinet).
If your video card requires additional power (via 6 or 8 pins), connect the power supply cable power cables to the corresponding connectors on the graphics card.


Connect the Front Panel Cables (Power Button, USB, LEDs, etc.)

Power button: The power button cable (usually marked "Power Switch") must be connected to the "PWR_SW" connector on the motherboard.
Front Panel LEDs: If your case has LEDs to indicate power or HDD status, connect the cables LED connector to the corresponding connector ("PWR_LED", "HDD_LED") on the motherboard.
USB ports: Connect the USB cables from the front panel of the case to the USB connectors on the motherboard. They normally They have 9 pins and are clearly identified.
Front panel audio: Connect the front panel audio cable to the "HD_AUDIO" connector on the motherboard.


Connect the Cooling Cables (Coolers and Fans)

Case fans: Connect the case fan cables to the "CHA_FAN" or "SYS_FAN" connectors on the motherboard.
Processor fan (CPU): The processor fan cable must be connected to the "CPU_FAN" connector on the motherboard.


Connect the Network Cable (Ethernet)

If you are using a wired connection, connect an Ethernet cable to your computer's network port and the other end on the router or network switch.
