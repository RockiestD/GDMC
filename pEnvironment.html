Preparing the Environment

First step:

To build a computer, the environment must be organized, clean and prepared to avoid damage to components. The work area needs to be spacious, with a stable, dust-free surface to ensure that the pieces are free from risks and have space to be handled safely.

Second step:

It is important to choose a dry location, away from sources of water or humidity, as liquids can cause short circuit and damage electronic components. The ambient temperature must be mild, without direct exposure to sunlight, to avoid overheating of parts during assembly.

Third step:

To avoid electrostatic discharges, which can damage circuits, it is recommended to work in anti-static surfaces or use grounded wrist straps. Avoid clothing made from fabrics that generate a lot of static electricity, such as wool or polyester, and if possible, touch a grounded metal surface before to manipulate the components.

Fourth step:

Finally, organize all the necessary tools and parts before you begin. Have screwdrivers and a container to keep screws and small parts safe. The preparation careful consideration of the environment facilitates assembly and protects the equipment, ensuring that the entire process is efficient and safe.
