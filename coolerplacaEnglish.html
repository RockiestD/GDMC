Placing the processor cooler on the motherboard

Placing the cooler on the motherboard is a simple process, but requires care. Here is a summary of the steps main:

Make sure you have the cooler box, the processor (CPU) already installed on the motherboard and that the thermal paste is applied (if it is not pre-applied to the cooler)
Check your motherboard manual to locate the cooler power connector (usually marked "CPU_FAN").

If the cooler does not have thermal paste pre-applied, apply a small amount (the size of a grain of rice) in the center of the processor. This paste helps transfer heat from the CPU to the cooler.

Align the retaining pins or screws on the cooler box with the holes around the CPU socket on the motherboard.
Make sure the cooler is correctly oriented so that the power cable easily reaches the "CPU_FAN" connector.


Attaching the cooler to the motherboard

For systems with push pins (such as Intel):

Press each pin down until it clicks, alternating between opposite sides (in an "X") to ensure even fixation.
Make sure all pins are securely seated.

For systems with screws (such as AMD or some Intel models):

Use a screwdriver to tighten the screws on the motherboard brackets. Tighten the screws crosswise ("X") to apply even pressure.


Connect the power cord

Locate the "CPU_FAN" connector on the motherboard and connect the cooler box cable.
Make sure the connector is securely seated, as it supplies power to the cooler.

Confirm that the cooler is securely attached and does not move when lightly touched.
Make sure the cooler's power cable is not obstructing the fan or other parts.
